Yotam Silberstein: A Global Jazz Guitar Experience

Wed, Jun 24

Yotam Silberstein is a globally acclaimed jazz guitarist whose sound fuses bebop, blues, and world rhythms into something vibrant, soulful, and completely unique.


Yotam’s acclaimed releases include "Universos" and "En el jardín," a collaboration with Carlos “Negro” Aguirre, and he is also a member of the John Patitucci Trio. He has toured with Brazilian icon Ivan Lins and appeared on notable recordings such as Monty Alexander’s GRAMMY-nominated "Harlem-Kingston Express Live!" and David Sanborn and Marcus Miller’s "Time and the River."


Silberstein has worked with jazz greats including George Coleman, Jimmy Heath, James Moody, Roy Hargrove, and Charles McPherson, and has performed at major festivals and venues worldwide such as the North Sea, Umbria, and Tokyo Jazz Festivals, as well as the Kennedy Center and Jazz at Lincoln Center. Born in Tel Aviv and based in New York, he was discovered by James Moody at a young age and has since earned praise from The New York Times and JazzTimes for his expressive playing and inventive compositions, drawing from a rich blend of global influences.

Doc's Presents: The Music of "Sinatra at the Sands" with Wyatt Michael and The Graeme Francis Orchestra

Residency

Sat, Jun 27

With a voice so close to Sinatra it's uncanny, Wyatt Michael (season 21 of "The Voice") joins forces with The Graeme Francis Orchestra to perform the groundbreaking album "Sinatra at the Sands" in its entirety, capturing the swagger, swing, and unmistakable charm of Sinatra’s golden era. Hear classics like "Come Fly With Me" and "Fly Me to the Moon," brought to life with bold brass, driving rhythms, and timeless energy that made the original performance unforgettable. Recorded live in 1966 at the Sands Hotel and Casino in Las Vegas, "Sinatra at the Sands" captured Frank Sinatra at his peak, backed by Count Basie’s powerhouse orchestra with arrangements conducted by Quincy Jones. As Sinatra’s first commercially released live album, it features many of the performances that came to define his signature sound and remains one of the most iconic live recordings in jazz history. Step into the golden age of Las Vegas and relive a night of classic swing, big band brilliance, and pure Sinatra style.
